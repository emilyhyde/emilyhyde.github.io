Emily Hyde

Aquatic microbial ecologist
with a love of all things archaeal,
weird & uncultured.

  • emilyhyde@utexas.edu

Broadly, my research spans biogeochemical cycling,
microbial communities in extreme environments, eukaryogenesis
syntrophy and the roles of uncultivated organisms in cycling.

My work is primarily computational, but I have extensive experience in the wet lab and the field, including research cruises.
I began my scientific journey at Oklahoma State University where I earned my B.S.A.G. in Environmental Science and gained my first experiences in research.
I moved north to University of Minnesota Duluth where I studied geomicrobiology and earned by M.S. in Water Resources Science.
Now, I am a Ecology, Evolution and Behavior PhD candidate in the Baker Marine Microbial Ecology and Evolution Lab at University of Texas at Austin.
